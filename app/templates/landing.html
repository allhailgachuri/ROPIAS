Traditional calendars told farmers:

Plant when the rains begin in March.

Climate change broke that calendar.

ROPIAS gives it back.

False onsets — brief early rains followed by devastating 14–21 day dry spells — routinely cost Kenyan smallholder farmers their entire seasonal investment in seeds, fertilizer, and labor. ROPIAS detects the statistical difference before the seed ever touches the ground.
